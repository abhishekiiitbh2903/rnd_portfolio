My IEEE ANTS 2025 Experience: Federated Learning for Phase Shift Optimization
Published: December 2025

I presented my research at IEEE ANTS 2025, where our work focused on applying federated learning to solve a challenging wireless communication problem involving phase shift optimization in multi-user scenarios. The conference provided an excellent platform to interact with researchers working at the intersection of machine learning and communication systems.

Research Focus: Federated Learning for Intelligent Communication Systems
Our paper addressed a core limitation of centralized optimization approaches in next-generation wireless networks. Specifically, we studied how to efficiently optimize phase shifts across multiple users while preserving data privacy and reducing communication overhead.

By leveraging a federated learning framework, we enabled distributed clients to collaboratively learn optimal phase configurations without sharing raw local data. This approach not only improved scalability in multi-user environments but also aligned well with privacy-aware network design principles essential for future communication systems.

Technical Highlights
• Federated optimization of phase shifts under heterogeneous user conditions
• Reduced communication overhead compared to centralized baselines
• Improved convergence stability in multi-user wireless settings
• Practical relevance for intelligent reflecting surfaces (IRS) and 6G networks

Memorable Interactions
One of the highlights of the conference was meeting Prof. Chandra R. Murthy . Our discussion on optimization challenges in wireless systems and the role of learning-based methods offered valuable insights and reinforced the real-world relevance of our work.

IEEE ANTS 2025 was a rewarding experience that strengthened my interest in applying machine learning to communication systems, particularly in distributed and privacy-preserving settings.
